# ZKP News - 2024-04

> **Note:** The following content was translated into English by AI.

### 2024.4.30

**[Papers]**

- 3MI Labs and Ulvetanna collaborate in the paper ‚ÄúVision Mark-32: ZK-Friendly Hash Function Over Binary Tower Fields,‚Äù proposing a special construction over binary tower fields and achieving a fully pipelined FPGA implementation. Experiments show an order-of-magnitude improvement over Poseidon hashing.
  *Note: The paper is also a good primer on binary tower fields.*
  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/633)

- Alqahtani et al. propose PBP-LEMs, a privacy-preserving billing protocol for local energy markets, in their paper ‚ÄúPrivacy-Preserving Billing for Local Energy Markets.‚Äù
  *Note: This is an example of ZKP in a real application scenario.*
  [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.15886)

- Sun et al. introduce zkLLM in ‚ÄúzkLLM: Zero Knowledge Proofs for Large Language Models,‚Äù a system that verifies the correctness of LLM outputs using the sumcheck protocol. It produces proofs under 200 kB and can attest to a 1.3B-parameter model within 15 minutes.
  [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.16109)

- Barthoulot et al. redefine cryptographic accumulators in ‚ÄúCryptographic Accumulators: New Definitions, Enhanced Security, and Delegatable Proofs,‚Äù adding a ‚Äúprivately evaluated unforgeability‚Äù property that bolsters security against forgery attacks.
  *Note: Accumulators can replace the widely used Merkle Tree in certain contexts, drawing significant attention.*
  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/657)

### 2024.4.17

**[Papers]**

- Kemmoe et al. publish ‚ÄúRSA-Based Dynamic Accumulator without Hashing into Primes,‚Äù optimizing dynamic accumulators that rely on RSA moduli rather than prime inputs.
  *Note: Traditional RSA accumulators require prime elements, making them expensive. Removing that requirement greatly improves performance. This paper extends the non-prime RSA accumulator from Boneh et al. with additional operations and proofs. Accumulators excel at membership proofs, a common ZKP use case.*
  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/505)

- Zhu et al. introduce ‚ÄúElastic MSM: A Fast, Elastic and Modular Preprocessing Technique for Multi-Scalar Multiplication Algorithm on GPUs,‚Äù describing GPU preprocessing that boosts MSM performance and potentially accelerates ZKP systems.
  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/057)

- Bottazzi from the Ethereum Foundation presents ‚ÄúGreco: Fast Zero-Knowledge Proofs for Valid FHE RLWE Ciphertexts Formation,‚Äù which uses ZKP to enhance the security and efficiency of multi-party FHE applications, such as proving the validity of encrypted ballots in anonymous voting.
  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/594)
  | [Code](https://github.com/privacy-scaling-explorations/greco)

- Yuan et al. revisit Fiat-Shamir signature security under quantum superposition attacks in ‚ÄúRevisiting the Security of Fiat-Shamir Signature Schemes under Superposition Attacks,‚Äù detailing how to maintain security for deterministic FS signatures in quantum settings.
  *Note: The FS transform underpins FS signatures and most modern **non-interactive** ZKPs.*
  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/590)

- Erfurth proposes a new digital signature scheme for compressed JPEG images in ‚ÄúDigital Signatures for Authenticating Compressed JPEG Images,‚Äù ensuring signatures remain valid even after compression.
  *Note: Proving the authenticity of images is increasingly important in the AI era.*
  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/588)

- Satriawan et al. offer ‚ÄúA Complete Beginner Guide to the Number Theoretic Transform (NTT),‚Äù covering NTT fundamentals, linear/circular/negacyclic convolutions, and practical examples.
  *Note: NTT is a foundational algorithm for many ZKP systems.*
  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/585)

- Sun et al. examine pairing optimizations for isogeny-based cryptosystems in ‚ÄúPairing Optimizations for Isogeny-based Cryptosystems,‚Äù addressing efficiency concerns in that post-quantum domain.
  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/575)

### 2024.4.10

**[Papers]**

- Robert presents a new algorithm in ‚ÄúFast pairings via biextensions and cubical arithmetic,‚Äù substantially improving pairing computations over existing methods like Miller‚Äôs algorithm. It unifies and extends several pairing techniques and accelerates pairings on specific elliptic curves.
  **This offers theoretical foundations for higher verifier efficiency in systems like GROTH16.**

- Victor Shoup analyzes Schnorr signatures in ‚ÄúThe many faces of Schnorr,‚Äù tightening security bounds and informing future threshold signature designs.

  [Paper](https://eprint.iacr.org/2023/1019)

- Alessandro Chiesa (co-author of Marlin) explores ‚ÄúA Time-Space Tradeoff for the Sumcheck Prover,‚Äù presenting a prover algorithm that optimizes both time and memory for sumcheck protocols over multilinear polynomials, with practical performance benefits.
